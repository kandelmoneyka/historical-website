Ramgram

Ramgram is a historical town in Nawalparasi district that is believed to be associated with Lord Buddha. It is said that when Lord Buddha was on his deathbed, he instructed his disciples to distribute his relics among eight powerful rulers of the time. One of those rulers was the king of Ramgram. The king built a stupa in Ramgram to enshrine the Buddha's relics, and the stupa has since become an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists.

Mythology

According to local mythology, Ramgram was the capital of the ancient kingdom of the same name. The kingdom was ruled by the descendants of Lord Ram, the hero of the Hindu epic Ramayana. It is said that Lord Ram's son, Luv, established the kingdom of Ramgram.

structure

The main attraction in Ramgram is the Ramgram Stupa, which is believed to enshrine a portion of Lord Buddha's relics. The stupa is about 43 meters high and 29 meters wide at the base. It is made of bricks and has a hemispherical dome at the top. The stupa is surrounded by a circular wall, and there are several smaller stupas and shrines in the surrounding area.

Getting There

Ramgram is located about 20 kilometers north of the city of Narayangarh in Chitwan district. The easiest way to get there is by taking a bus or taxi from Narayangarh. The nearest airport is in Bharatpur, which is about 25 kilometers away.
