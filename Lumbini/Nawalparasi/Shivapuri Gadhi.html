Shivapuri Gadhi

Shivapuri Gadhi is a historical fort located in Nawalparasi district of Nepal. Here's some information on its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

Shivapuri Gadhi is believed to have been built during the reign of King Prithvi Narayan Shah, who is known for unifying modern-day Nepal. The fort was strategically built to defend the western region of Nepal from the British East India Company's invasion.

Mythology

According to local mythology, Lord Shiva meditated in the area where Shivapuri Gadhi is located. It is believed that the fort was built on top of the spot where Lord Shiva sat during his meditation.

Structure

Shivapuri Gadhi is a hill fort located at an altitude of 1,327 meters above sea level. The fort was strategically built on top of a hill, providing a clear view of the surrounding area. The fort's structure includes a palace, temples, and a water reservoir.

Getting There

Shivapuri Gadhi is located in Nawalparasi district, which is approximately 180 km west of Kathmandu, the capital city of Nepal. To reach the fort, you can take a bus or hire a taxi from Kathmandu to reach Nawalparasi. From there, you can hike up to the fort or hire a local guide to take you there. The hike to Shivapuri Gadhi takes approximately 2-3 hours and offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
