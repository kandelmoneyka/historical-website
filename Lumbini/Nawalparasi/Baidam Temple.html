Baidam Temple

Baidam Temple is one of the ancient temple ruins located in Nawalparasi district, Nepal. Here is some information on the history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The history of the Baidam temple dates back to the 15th century during the Malla dynasty in Nepal. The temple was constructed during the reign of King Hari Simha Deva. It was used for various religious purposes, and people from far and wide used to come to this temple to offer their prayers.

Mythology

According to local legends, the Baidam temple was built by a group of demons who were defeated by the goddess Durga. These demons had been creating havoc and destruction in the surrounding area, and the goddess Durga was called upon to put an end to their reign of terror. After defeating the demons, the goddess ordered the construction of the Baidam temple on the site of the battle.

Structure

The Baidam temple is a beautiful example of traditional Newari architecture. The temple is made of red bricks and features intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple has three tiers, and each tier has a different style of architecture. The lower tier features intricate carvings, while the middle tier has ornate statues of gods and goddesses. The upper tier has a dome-shaped structure and is decorated with colorful prayer flags.

Getting There

The Baidam temple is located in Nawalparasi district, which is about 150 km west of Kathmandu. The nearest airport is the Gautam Buddha Airport in Bhairahawa, which is about 30 km away from the temple. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to the temple. It is also possible to take a private car or motorcycle to explore the temple at your own pace.
