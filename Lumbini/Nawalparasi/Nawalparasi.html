NawalparasiNawalparasi District is a district in the Lumbini Province of Nepal. It is located in the southern part of Nepal, bordering India to the south. The district covers an area of 2,162 square kilometers and has a population of approximately 643,508 people, according to the 2011 Nepal census.
The district is divided into two parts: Nawalparasi East and Nawalparasi West. The administrative center of Nawalparasi East is Kawasoti, while the administrative center of Nawalparasi West is Parasi. The major ethnic groups living in the district are Brahmin, Chhetri, Magar, Tharu, and Newar.
Agriculture is the main occupation of the people in the district. The major crops grown in the district include rice, maize, wheat, and sugarcane. The district is also known for its production of mustard oil and ghee.
The district has several tourist attractions, including the Bish Hazari Lake, the Chitwan National Park, and the Devghat pilgrimage site. The district is also known for its traditional handicrafts, such as bamboo and cane products, and for its religious festivals, such as the Maghe Sankranti and the Teej festival.
