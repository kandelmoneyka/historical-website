Baglamukhi Temple

Baglamukhi Temple is located in the Bardiya district of Nepal. It is a popular Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Baglamukhi, who is believed to have the power to ward off evil spirits and negative energies. Here is some information about the history, mythology, structure, and getting there:

History

The exact history of the Baglamukhi Temple is not clear, but it is believed to have been built during the reign of the Malla dynasty, which ruled Nepal from the 12th to the 18th century. The temple has undergone several renovations over the years.

Mythology

The temple is dedicated to Goddess Baglamukhi, who is believed to be an incarnation of Goddess Durga. According to Hindu mythology, Baglamukhi is known for her ability to stop or paralyze her enemies. Devotees come to the temple to seek her blessings to overcome obstacles and defeat enemies.

Structure

The Baglamukhi Temple is a small temple with a unique architecture. The temple has a dome-shaped roof and a square base. The walls of the temple are made of mud and bricks, and the roof is made of copper. The temple has a small porch and a sanctum sanctorum where the idol of Baglamukhi is placed. The idol of Baglamukhi is made of black stone and is decorated with gold and silver ornaments.

The Baglamukhi Temple is located in Bardiya, which is about 400 kilometers from Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal. The nearest airport is the Nepalgunj airport, which is about 90 kilometers away from the temple. From Nepalgunj, one can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the temple.
