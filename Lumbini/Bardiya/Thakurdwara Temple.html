Thakurdwara Temple

Thakurdwara is a temple located in Bardiya district of Nepal. The temple holds great significance among the people of Bardiya district and attracts a large number of pilgrims every year.

History

The exact history of Thakurdwara is not well documented. However, it is believed that the temple was built during the Malla period, which ruled the region between the 12th and 18th century. According to local folklore, the temple was built by a local king who was a devotee of Lord Vishnu.

Mythology

The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is one of the most important deities in Hinduism. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Vishnu is the preserver of the universe and is believed to have taken ten incarnations to protect the world from evil forces. The Thakurdwara temple is believed to be one of the few places in Nepal where Lord Vishnu is worshipped in his "Lakshmi-Narayan" form, which represents the combined form of Lord Vishnu and his consort, Goddess Lakshmi.

Structure

The Thakurdwara temple is a beautiful example of traditional Nepalese architecture. The temple is made of red bricks and features intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple has a spacious courtyard, which is surrounded by several smaller shrines dedicated to other Hindu deities. The main temple is a three-story structure with a pagoda-style roof, which is adorned with golden pinnacle.

Getting There

Thakurdwara is located in Bardiya district of Nepal, which is around 400 km west of Kathmandu. The nearest airport is located in Nepalgunj, which is around 90 km away from the temple. From Nepalgunj, one can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the temple. The temple can also be reached by taking a direct bus from Kathmandu or other major cities in Nepal.
