Bardiya is a district located in the Province No. 5 of Nepal. It covers an area of 2,025 square kilometers and has a population of approximately 300,000 people. Bardia is a district that is renowned for its natural beauty and wildlife, and has become a popular destination for tourists in recent years.Bardiya district is known for its diverse wildlife and natural beauty. The Bardiya National Park, one of the largest national parks in Nepal, is located in the district and is home to a variety of endangered species, including Bengal tigers, one-horned rhinoceroses, and Asian elephants.
The majority of the population in Bardiya district are Tharu people, an ethnic group indigenous to the Terai region of Nepal. Agriculture is the main occupation of the people, with rice, wheat, and maize being the major crops grown in the district. The district also has potential for tourism, particularly ecotourism due to its natural beauty and wildlife.
