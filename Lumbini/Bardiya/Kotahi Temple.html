Kotahi Temple

The Kotahi Temple of Bardiya is a significant Hindu pilgrimage site located in the Bardiya district of Nepal. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism, and is renowned for its historical, mythological, and architectural significance.

History

The exact origins of the Kotahi Temple are unknown, but according to local folklore, it was built around 600 years ago by a king named Mukunda Sen. The temple has undergone several renovations over the centuries, the most recent of which was completed in 2011 after the temple was damaged by a massive flood in the region.

Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, the Kotahi Temple is located at the spot where Lord Shiva, the god of destruction and transformation, appeared in the form of a lingam (a symbolic representation of his divine energy). The temple is also believed to have been the site where the Pandavas, the heroes of the Hindu epic Mahabharata, offered their prayers to Lord Shiva.

Structure

The Kotahi Temple is a beautiful example of Nepalese temple architecture. It is constructed from stone and brick and features intricate carvings of various Hindu deities, including Lord Shiva, on its exterior walls. The temple has three main entrances, each adorned with a towering gajur (a type of ornamental wooden pole) and a pair of stone lions. The sanctum sanctorum of the temple houses the lingam of Lord Shiva, which is the main focus of worship.

Getting There

The Kotahi Temple is located approximately 6 kilometers south of the city of Gulariya in the Bardiya district of Nepal. Visitors can reach the temple by road or by taking a short trek through the surrounding forest. The temple is open to visitors of all faiths, and it is particularly popular among Hindu devotees during the festival of Maha Shivaratri, which usually falls in February or March.
