Niglihawa

Niglihawa is a village located in the Kapilvastu district of Nepal. It is a site of great historical and religious significance, as it is believed to be the birthplace of Kanakamuni Buddha, one of the previous Buddhas before Gautama Buddha. Here's some information on Niglihawa:

History and Mythology

According to Buddhist legends, Kanakamuni Buddha was born in Niglihawa and achieved enlightenment at the site of the village's stupa. The village was also a prominent center for Buddhism during the time of Gautama Buddha. Archaeological findings have also revealed the presence of several ancient stupas and pillars in the area, indicating the site's significance in ancient times.

Structure

The main attraction of Niglihawa is the ancient stupa believed to have been built by Kanakamuni Buddha himself. The stupa is surrounded by other smaller stupas and ruins of ancient structures. The site has been excavated by archaeologists, and several important artifacts have been found here.

Getting There

Niglihawa is located about 10 km southwest of Tilaurakot, the ancient capital of Kapilvastu. The nearest airport is in Bhairahawa, which is about 35 km away. From Bhairahawa, one can take a taxi or a bus to reach Niglihawa. The site is also accessible from other nearby towns and villages by road. In conclusion, Niglihawa is a site of great historical and religious significance for Buddhists. The ancient stupa and other ruins make it an important archaeological site as well.
