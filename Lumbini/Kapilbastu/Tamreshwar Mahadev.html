Tamreshwar Mahadev

Tamreshwar Mahadev is a Hindu temple located in Kapilvastu district of Nepal. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is known as Tamreshwar Mahadev among the locals. Here is some information about the history, mythology, structure, and getting there of Tamreshwar Mahadev:

History

The exact history of Tamreshwar Mahadev temple is not known. However, according to local legends, the temple was built by Lord Rama during his exile period. Another legend states that the temple was built by a devotee of Lord Shiva who had a vision of the deity in his dream.

Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva is one of the major gods in the Hindu pantheon. He is known as the destroyer of evil and the god of creation, destruction, and transformation. Tamreshwar Mahadev is considered to be one of the most important temples dedicated to Lord Shiva in Nepal. It is believed that Lord Shiva himself resides in the temple in the form of a lingam.

Structure

The Tamreshwar Mahadev temple is a small, single-story structure with a square-shaped sanctum sanctorum (garbha griha) that houses the lingam. The temple is built in the traditional pagoda style of Nepalese architecture, with a sloping roof and wooden eaves. The temple is adorned with intricate carvings and statues of various deities.

Getting There

Tamreshwar Mahadev temple is located in the Kapilvastu district of Nepal, near the Indian border. The nearest town is Krishnanagar, which is about 4 kilometers away from the temple. The nearest airport is the Gautam Buddha Airport in Bhairahawa, which is about 50 kilometers away from the temple. The temple is easily accessible by road and is well connected to major cities in Nepal. Visitors can also hire a taxi or take a bus from nearby towns to reach the temple.
