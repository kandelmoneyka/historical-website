Tilaurakot

Tilaurakot is an archaeological site located in Kapilvastu district of Nepal. It is believed to be the site of the ancient city of Kapilavastu, which was the capital of the Sakya Kingdom and the childhood home of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became the Buddha.

History and Mithology

According to Buddhist tradition, Siddhartha Gautama was born in Lumbini, which is located a few kilometers west of Tilaurakot. He spent the first 29 years of his life in Kapilavastu, where he lived a luxurious life in the palace. However, he left the palace in search of enlightenment and eventually became the Buddha.

Archaeological Structure

Tilaurakot is an extensive archaeological site that covers an area of around 25 square kilometers. It is surrounded by a moat and a wall made of mud and brick. The city was divided into several sections, including the royal palace, the city center, and the residential area. The palace is believed to have been the residence of King Suddhodana, the father of Siddhartha Gautama.

Getting There

Tilaurakot is located about 27 kilometers from Lumbini, the birthplace of the Buddha. The easiest way to get there is by car or taxi, which takes around 45 minutes to an hour. There are also buses and shared taxis that run between Lumbini and Tilaurakot.
