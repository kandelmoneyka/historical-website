Charpala Durbar

Charpala Durbar is an ancient palace located in the district of Gulmi in Nepal. It is a historical site that has a rich history and mythology associated with it. Here's an overview of its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The exact date of the construction of Charpala Durbar is unknown. However, it is believed to have been built during the reign of King Mukunda Sen of Palpa in the 16th century. The palace served as a residence for the royal family of Palpa for many years. It was later abandoned after the downfall of the Palpa Kingdom.

Mythology

Charpala Durbar is also associated with a popular mythological story. According to the legend, a group of four brothers named Charpala once lived in the palace. They were believed to be supernatural beings with extraordinary powers. It is said that they protected the palace from evil spirits and kept the people of Palpa safe.

Structure

Charpala Durbar is a unique structure that reflects the architecture of the medieval era. The palace is built using stones and mud bricks. It has four wings, each with a separate entrance. The palace has a central courtyard that connects all the wings. The palace also has a watchtower that was used to keep an eye on the surroundings.

Getting There

Charpala Durbar is located in the district of Gulmi, which is approximately 220 km from Kathmandu. The nearest town to the palace is Tamghas, which is about 7 km away. From Tamghas, visitors can hire a taxi or a jeep to reach Charpala Durbar. The palace is situated on top of a hill, and visitors need to climb a steep path to reach it.
