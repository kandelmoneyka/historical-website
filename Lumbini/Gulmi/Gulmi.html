Gulmi Gulmi is a district located in the western part of Nepal. It is part of the Lumbini Province and covers an area of 1,149 square kilometers. The district headquarters is Tamghas, which is also the largest town in the district.
Gulmi is known for its natural beauty and is home to several mountain ranges, including the Dhaulagiri and Annapurna ranges. The district is also known for its agriculture, particularly the production of coffee and tea.
Some of the notable tourist attractions in Gulmi include the Ruru Kshetra pilgrimage site, the historic Ridi Bazaar, and the scenic views from the top of the Satyawati hill. The district is also home to several ethnic groups, including the Magars, Gurungs, and Brahmins.
