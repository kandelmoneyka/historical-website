Resunga

Resunga is a popular pilgrimage site located in the Gulmi district of Nepal. It is famous for its religious, historical, and mythological significance. Here's some information about Resunga of Gulmi:

History

Resunga has a rich history that dates back to ancient times. It is believed that the area was ruled by various kings and dynasties, and it was an important center for trade and commerce. The area has also witnessed many battles and wars, and it played a significant role in the unification of Nepal under King Prithvi Narayan Shah.

Mythology

According to local legends, Resunga is the abode of the Hindu goddess Bhagwati, who is believed to have the power to grant wishes and fulfill desires. It is said that the goddess appeared in a dream to a local farmer, who later found a sacred stone that became the basis of the Resunga Temple.

Structure

The Resunga Temple is the main attraction of the area. It is a beautifully crafted pagoda-style temple that is dedicated to the goddess Bhagwati. The temple is located at the top of a hill and offers a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding hills and valleys. The temple is surrounded by other smaller temples, and there are also many stone carvings and sculptures that can be found in the area.

Getting There

Resunga is located in the Gulmi district of Nepal, which is about a 6-hour drive from Kathmandu. The nearest airport is in Bhairahawa, which is about a 2-hour drive away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Gulmi and then hike up to Resunga. The hike takes about 3-4 hours and can be quite steep, so visitors should be prepared for a challenging climb. Alternatively, visitors can hire a jeep or a motorcycle to reach the temple.
