Rishi Keshav Temple

Rishikesh Temple is a historic temple located in the Palpa district of Nepal. Here is some information on the history, mythology, structure, and how to get to the Rishikesh Temple:

History

The Rishikesh Temple is believed to have been built in the 17th century by the King of Palpa, Mukunda Sen. The temple has undergone several renovations over the centuries, the most recent one being in 2011

Mythology

According to local mythology, the temple is associated with Lord Rama, the hero of the Indian epic, Ramayana. It is believed that Lord Rama visited this place during his 14-year exile and stayed here for some time. The name Rishikesh is derived from the name of Lord Rama, who is also known as Rishikesha, meaning "the Lord of the senses."

Structure

The Rishikesh Temple is a pagoda-style temple built in the traditional Nepali architectural style. The temple is made of stone and has a multi-tiered roof with a golden pinnacle. The main deity of the temple is Lord Rishikesh (Lord Vishnu), who is flanked by Lord Shiva and Lord Brahma.

Getting There

The Rishikesh Temple is located in the town of Tansen, which is the administrative headquarters of the Palpa district. Tansen is well-connected by road and can be reached by bus or taxi from major cities in Nepal. The nearest airport is the Gautam Buddha Airport in Bhairahawa, which is about 75 km away from Tansen. The temple is located on a hill and can be reached by climbing a flight of stairs from the town center. In conclusion, the Rishikesh Temple is a historic and sacred place in the Palpa district of Nepal. Its association with Lord Rama and its beautiful architecture make it a must-visit destination for tourists and devotees alike.
