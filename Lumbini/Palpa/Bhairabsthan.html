Bhairabsthan

Bhairabsthan Temple is an ancient Hindu temple located in the Palpa district of Nepal. It is dedicated to Lord Bhairav, who is considered to be an incarnation of Lord Shiva. The temple is situated on the banks of the Kali Gandaki River, which is a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists.

History And Mithology

The history and mythology of Bhairabsthan Temple are closely intertwined. According to local legend, the temple was established in the 17th century by a saint named Bhavani Nath, who was a devotee of Lord Bhairav. He built the temple on the site where he had a vision of Lord Bhairav, who instructed him to establish a temple in his honor.The temple is also associated with the famous Hindu epic, the Mahabharata. According to the legend, it is believed that the Pandavas, who were the heroes of the Mahabharata, visited this place during their exile. It is said that the Pandavas offered prayers at this temple and sought the blessings of Lord Bhairav.

Structure

The temple has a traditional pagoda-style structure and is made of stone and wood. The roof of the temple is made of copper and is supported by wooden pillars. The main entrance of the temple is adorned with intricate carvings of Hindu deities. The inner sanctum of the temple houses a statue of Lord Bhairav, which is made of black stone. The statue is adorned with jewelry and is surrounded by other idols of Hindu deities. The temple also has a small courtyard where devotees can offer prayers and perform rituals.

Getting There

Bhairabsthan Temple is located in the Palpa district of Nepal, which is approximately 300 kilometers from the capital city of Kathmandu. The nearest airport is located in Pokhara, which is approximately 80 kilometers from the temple. The temple is easily accessible by road, and buses and taxis are available from nearby towns and cities. The best time to visit the temple is during the festival of Navratri, which is celebrated in October or November.
