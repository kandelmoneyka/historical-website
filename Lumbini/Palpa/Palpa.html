Palpa

Palpa is a district located in the western region of Nepal. It is part of the Lumbini Province and covers an area of 1,373 square kilometers. The district is surrounded by the districts of Gulmi to the east, Arghakhanchi and Kapilvastu to the west, Syangja to the north, and Rupandehi to the south. The district headquarters of Palpa is Tansen, which is also one of the major cities in the region. Other major towns in the district include Rampur, Rambha, and Nisdi. Palpa is known for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance. The district has numerous temples, palaces, and monuments that date back to the medieval period. Some of the popular tourist attractions in Palpa include the Tansen Durbar, Shreenagar Hill, Ridi Bazaar, and the Ranighat Palace. Overall, Palpa is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical significance, making it a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Nepal.
