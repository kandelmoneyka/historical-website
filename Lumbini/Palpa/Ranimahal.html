Rani Mahal

The Rani Mahal of Palpa is a historical palace located in the town of Tansen in the Palpa district of Nepal. It is a popular tourist attraction due to its rich history and unique architecture. Here is some information about its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The Rani Mahal was built in the 19th century by Khadga Shumsher Rana as a palace for his beloved queen Tej Kumari. However, the queen passed away before the palace was completed. After her death, the palace remained unused for many years until it was converted into a government office. During the Maoist insurgency in Nepal, the palace was damaged by fire and now stands as a partially ruined structure.

Mythology

There are many myths associated with the Rani Mahal, one of which is that it was built on the site where the goddess Parvati appeared to a local farmer. According to the myth, the goddess promised the farmer that she would come back to the same spot in the future, and that is why the palace was built there.

Structure

The Rani Mahal is a mix of Mughal and European architectural styles. It has a red-brick facade with white accents and intricate carvings. The palace has two courtyards, one of which has a fountain in the center. The palace also has a tower from where visitors can get a panoramic view of Tansen town.

Getting There

The Rani Mahal is located in Tansen, which is about 5 hours by bus from Kathmandu. There are also daily flights from Kathmandu to Pokhara, which is about 3 hours away from Tansen by bus. Once you reach Tansen, the palace is a short walk from the town center. Overall, the Rani Mahal of Palpa is a fascinating historical structure that is worth a visit if you are interested in Nepali history and architecture.
