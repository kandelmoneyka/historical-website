Baraha Temple

Baraha Temple is one of the most important religious sites in Dang district, located in the village of Nawalpur, around 20 kilometers from the district headquarters Ghorahi. Here is some information on its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The history of Baraha Temple dates back to the 17th century when it was built by a local king named Mukunda Sen. The temple was constructed to worship Lord Vishnu in his avatar as Baraha (boar) and is believed to have been built on the exact spot where Lord Baraha is said to have appeared.

Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, Lord Baraha is believed to have appeared on earth to rescue the earth goddess, Bhudevi, from the demon Hiranyaksha. It is believed that the demon had taken the earth goddess into the depths of the ocean, and Lord Baraha rescued her by killing the demon.

Structure

The Baraha Temple is a beautiful example of traditional Newari architecture, with intricate carvings and decorations. The temple is situated on a raised platform and is surrounded by a spacious courtyard. The main shrine of the temple houses an image of Lord Baraha, made of black stone, which is around 2 feet tall. The temple also has several other smaller shrines dedicated to other Hindu deities.

Getting There

The Baraha Temple is located in the village of Nawalpur, which is around 20 kilometers from the district headquarters Ghorahi. The easiest way to get to the temple is by hiring a taxi or a private vehicle from Ghorahi. It takes around 45 minutes to reach the temple from Ghorahi. There are also several local buses that operate between Ghorahi and Nawalpur, which can be an affordable option for those traveling on a budget.
