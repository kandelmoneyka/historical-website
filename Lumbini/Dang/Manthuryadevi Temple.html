Manthuryadevi Temple

Manthuryadevi Temple is a famous religious site located in the village of Manthali in Dang district of Nepal. Here is some information on its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The exact history of the Manthuryadevi Temple is not known. However, it is believed that the temple was built around 500 years ago during the reign of the Sen dynasty. The temple was later renovated in the 19th century by the Rana dynasty. It is believed that the temple was built to honor Goddess Manthuryadevi, who is considered to be a powerful goddess in the region.

Mythology

According to local mythology, Goddess Manthuryadevi is believed to be an incarnation of Goddess Kali. It is believed that she came to the village of Manthali to help the people and protect them from evil spirits. The goddess is worshipped by the locals for her ability to cure diseases and bless them with good health and prosperity.

Structure

The Manthuryadevi Temple is a beautiful example of traditional Nepali architecture, with intricate carvings and decorations. The temple is situated on a raised platform and is surrounded by a spacious courtyard. The main shrine of the temple houses an image of Goddess Manthuryadevi, made of black stone, which is around 2 feet tall. The temple also has several other smaller shrines dedicated to other Hindu deities.

Getting There

The Manthuryadevi Temple is located in the village of Manthali, which is around 10 kilometers from the district headquarters Ghorahi. The easiest way to get to the temple is by hiring a taxi or a private vehicle from Ghorahi. It takes around 30 minutes to reach the temple from Ghorahi. There are also several local buses that operate between Ghorahi and Manthali, which can be an affordable option for those traveling on a budget.
