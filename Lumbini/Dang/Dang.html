Dang

Dang is a district located in the Province No. 5 of Nepal. It covers an area of 2,955 square kilometers and has a population of approximately 550,000 people. Dang is a diverse district with a rich cultural heritage, a strong agricultural economy, and several popular tourist destinations.Dang district is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and diverse ethnic groups. The district is home to various ethnic groups, including Tharu, Magar, Brahmin, Chhetri, and Newar. The majority of the people in the district follow Hinduism, while a significant minority follows Buddhism and Islam. The district is blessed with natural beauty, including the scenic Rapti River, which flows through the district, and the beautiful forests of the Chure Hills. The district is also home to several national parks, including the Bardia National Park and the Suklaphanta National Park, which are famous for their wildlife, including tigers, elephants, rhinos, and many other species. The economy of the district is primarily dependent on agriculture, with rice, maize, wheat, and vegetables being the major crops. Besides agriculture, industries like cement, bricks, and tiles production, and service sector including healthcare and education are also important for the district's economy. In conclusion, Dang district is a beautiful and culturally diverse district in Nepal with a rich heritage, natural beauty, and a diverse economy.
