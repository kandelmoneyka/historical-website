Gorakhnath Temple

Gorakhnath Temple is another important religious site in Dang district, located in the village of Gorakhpur, around 16 kilometers from the district headquarters Ghorahi. Here is some information on its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

Histoty

The history of Gorakhnath Temple dates back to the 12th century when it was built by the famous yogi, Guru Gorakhnath. Guru Gorakhnath was a renowned saint who is said to have meditated at the site where the temple is located. The temple was later rebuilt by King Mukunda Sen in the 17th century and has since been an important pilgrimage site for both Hindus and Buddhists.

Mythology

According to local folklore, Guru Gorakhnath meditated at the site of the temple for several years, during which time he performed many miracles and is said to have attained enlightenment. It is also believed that the temple is located on a spot where Lord Shiva is said to have appeared in the form of a lingam (phallic symbol).

Structure

The Gorakhnath Temple is a beautiful example of traditional Newari architecture, with intricate carvings and decorations. The temple is situated on a raised platform and is surrounded by a spacious courtyard. The main shrine of the temple houses an image of Guru Gorakhnath, made of black stone, which is around 3 feet tall. The temple also has several other smaller shrines dedicated to other Hindu deities.

Getting There

The Gorakhnath Temple is located in the village of Gorakhpur, which is around 16 kilometers from the district headquarters Ghorahi. The easiest way to get to the temple is by hiring a taxi or a private vehicle from Ghorahi. It takes around 30 minutes to reach the temple from Ghorahi. There are also several local buses that operate between Ghorahi and Gorakhpur, which can be an affordable option for those traveling on a budget.
