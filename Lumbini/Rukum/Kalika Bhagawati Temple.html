Kalika Bhagawati Temple

There are several Kalika Bhagwati Temples in Nepal, but one of the significant temples with this name is located in Rukum district. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Kali, who is considered the goddess of time, change, and destruction.

History and Mythology

The temple is believed to have been built in the 16th century by the local Kham Magar people. According to local mythology, the temple was built to honor the goddess Kali, who is believed to have saved the people of the region from a series of calamities and disasters. As per the legend, the goddess Kali appeared in a dream to a local king, and instructed him to build a temple in her honor. The king followed the instructions, and the temple was built at the spot where the goddess had appeared in his dream.

Structure

The Kalika Bhagwati Temple is a beautiful pagoda-style structure, which is decorated with intricate carvings and paintings. The temple has a golden roof, which shines brightly in the sunlight. The inner sanctum of the temple houses a black stone image of Kali, which is adorned with flowers and ornaments.

Getting There

Kalika Bhagwati Temple is located in the Kotjahari Rural Municipality of Rukum district. The temple can be reached by a short trek from the district headquarters of Musikot. The trek to the temple takes around two hours, and the trail passes through beautiful forests and scenic landscapes. Alternatively, visitors can also take a private vehicle or a public bus to the nearby town of Rangsi, which is around 5 kilometers from the temple. From Rangsi, visitors can take a short hike to the temple. Overall, the Kalika Bhagwati Temple is a significant religious and cultural site in Rukum district, Nepal, and is a must-visit destination for those interested in the local history and mythology of the region.
