Dhuwakot Temple

Dhuwakot Temple is a revered Hindu temple located in Rukum district, Nepal. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Durga, who is considered the goddess of power, prosperity, and good fortune.

History and Mythology

The temple is believed to have been built in the 15th century by the local Kham Magar people. According to local mythology, the temple was built to honor a powerful goddess who helped the people of the region overcome a devastating famine.The temple also has a significant historical value, as it is believed to have played a key role in the unification of Nepal in the 18th century. It is said that King Prithvi Narayan Shah, the founder of modern Nepal, received blessings from the goddess Durga at the Dhuwakot Temple before embarking on his mission to unify the country.

Structure

The Dhuwakot Temple is a beautiful pagoda-style structure, which is adorned with intricate carvings and colorful paintings. The temple has a golden roof, which shines brightly in the sunlight. The inner sanctum of the temple houses a black stone image of the goddess Durga, which is decorated with flowers and ornaments.

Getting There

Dhuwakot Temple is located in the Dhuwakot Rural Municipality of Rukum district. The temple can be reached by a short trek from the district headquarters of Musikot. The trek to the temple takes around two hours, and the trail passes through beautiful forests and scenic landscapes. Alternatively, visitors can also take a private vehicle or a public bus to the nearby town of Sisne, which is around 5 kilometers from the temple. From Sisne, visitors can take a short hike to the temple. Overall, the Dhuwakot Temple is a significant religious and cultural site in Rukum district, Nepal, and is a must-visit destination for those interested in the local history and mythology of the region.
