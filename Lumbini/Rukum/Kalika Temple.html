Kalika Temple

Kalika Temple is a significant religious site located in Rukum district, Nepal. It is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Kali, who is considered the goddess of time, change, and destruction.

History and Mythology

The temple has a significant religious and historical value. According to local beliefs, the temple was constructed by the local Kham Magar people in the 16th century. The temple is believed to have been built to protect the people of the region from evil spirits and other negative influences. As per the mythology, the temple is said to have been built over the spot where the goddess Kali killed a demon named Raktabija. The demon was believed to be invincible as every time a drop of his blood fell on the ground, a new demon was born. To defeat him, Kali drank his blood and killed him.

Structure

The temple is a beautiful pagoda-style structure, which is decorated with intricate carvings and paintings. The temple has a golden roof, which shines brightly in the sunlight. The inner sanctum of the temple houses a black stone image of Kali, which is adorned with flowers and ornaments.

Getting There

Kalika Temple is located in the Kalika Rural Municipality of Rukum district. The temple can be reached by a short trek from the district headquarters of Musikot. The trek to the temple takes around two hours, and the trail passes through beautiful forests and scenic landscapes. Alternatively, visitors can also take a private vehicle or a public bus to the nearby town of Bafikot, which is around 10 kilometers from the temple. From Bafikot, visitors can take a short hike to the temple. Overall, the Kalika Temple is a significant religious and cultural site in Rukum district, Nepal, and is a must-visit destination for those interested in the local history and mythology of the region.
