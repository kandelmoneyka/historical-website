Lumbini

Lumbini is a small town in the southwestern Terai plains of Nepal, located approximately 300 kilometers west of Kathmandu and 170 kilometers south of Pokhara. It is renowned as the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, the founder of Buddhism and later known as the Buddha.

History And Mythology

According to Buddhist tradition, Queen Mahamayadevi gave birth to Prince Siddhartha in Lumbini in 623 BCE. The child was destined to become the Buddha, the enlightened one who would teach the path to liberation from suffering. The sacred site of Lumbini was rediscovered in the late 19th century by the Nepalese archaeologist Khadga Shamsher Rana.

Structure of Temple

Lumbini is home to many temples and monuments, including the Maya Devi Temple, which is the main temple at the site. The temple is situated near the sacred pond where Queen Mahamayadevi is believed to have given birth to the Buddha. The temple houses a stone slab which marks the exact spot where the Buddha was born. The temple complex also includes several other shrines and a museum.

Getting there

The nearest airport to Lumbini is the Gautam Buddha Airport in Bhairahawa, which is approximately 22 kilometers away. Regular flights operate from Kathmandu and other major cities in Nepal. Alternatively, Lumbini can also be reached by road. Buses and taxis are available from Kathmandu, Pokhara, and other major cities in Nepal. The journey by road can take anywhere from 6 to 10 hours, depending on the mode of transport and the route taken.
