Chhatra Maharaj Temple

Chhatra Maharaj Temple is a popular Hindu temple located in the Arghakhachi district of Nepal. Here's some information on the history, mythology, structure of the temple, and getting there:

History

The history of the Chhatra Maharaj Temple is not very well documented. However, it is believed to have been built in the 17th century during the reign of King Ram Shah of the Palpa Kingdom. The temple was built in honor of the Hindu deity Chhatra Maharaj, who is worshipped for his protection, knowledge, and guidance

Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, Chhatra Maharaj is a deity who provides protection, knowledge, and guidance to his devotees. He is also known as a deity of kingship and is often depicted with a crown on his head. Chhatra Maharaj is worshipped by many in Nepal, particularly in the Arghakhachi district.

Struture Of Temple

The Chhatra Maharaj Temple is a traditional Newari-style temple, which is common in Nepal. The temple has a three-tiered roof and is made of brick and wood. The main idol of Chhatra Maharaj is housed in a small chamber inside the temple.

Getting There

The Chhatra Maharaj Temple is located in the town of Khanchikot in the Arghakhachi district of Nepal. The easiest way to get there is by taking a bus or car from the nearby city of Butwal. The temple is located about 20 kilometers from Butwal and can be reached in about 45 minutes by road. In conclusion, the Chhatra Maharaj Temple in Arghakhachi district is a popular Hindu temple with a rich history and mythology. The traditional Newari-style temple is a beautiful sight to see, and getting there is relatively easy by bus or car from nearby Butwal.
