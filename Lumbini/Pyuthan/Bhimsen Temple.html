Bhimsen temple

Bhimsen Temple of Pyuthan is a significant religious site located in the Pyuthan district of Nepal. Here is some information on its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The Bhimsen Temple of Pyuthan is believed to have been built during the reign of King Siddhinarsingh Malla of Pyuthan in the 15th century. It was later renovated during the Rana regime in the early 20th century. The temple has been an important pilgrimage site for the locals of Pyuthan district for many centuries.

Mythology

The temple is dedicated to the Hindu deity Bhimsen, who is considered to be an avatar of Lord Vishnu. According to Hindu mythology, Bhimsen was one of the five Pandava brothers in the epic Mahabharata. The temple is believed to have been built at the site where Bhimsen had rested during his travels in the region.

Structure

The Bhimsen Temple of Pyuthan is a pagoda-style temple that is constructed with wood, stone, and brick. The temple has a rectangular base and a tall, pointed roof. It is adorned with intricate wood carvings and metal ornaments. The temple also has a separate structure dedicated to the goddess Taleju, who is considered to be the protector of the temple.

Getting There

The Bhimsen Temple of Pyuthan is located in the center of Pyuthan Bazaar in the Pyuthan district of Nepal. Pyuthan can be reached by bus or jeep from Kathmandu or Pokhara. From Pyuthan Bazaar, the temple is a short walk away. Overall, the Bhimsen Temple of Pyuthan is a beautiful and significant religious site that is worth visiting for anyone interested in Hindu mythology and Nepali culture.
