Sworgadwari Temple

History

Sworgadwari Temple is an ancient temple located in Pyuthan district of Nepal. It is believed that the temple was built during the reign of the Licchavi dynasty in the 5th or 6th century AD. According to mythology, the Pandavas from the Mahabharata epic visited this place during their exile period. The temple is also mentioned in the Skanda Purana, one of the Hindu scriptures.

Mythology

There is a popular belief among the devotees that if one visits the temple, they will attain salvation or Sworga (heaven). The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is believed to be one of the 108 Divya Desams (sacred places) of Lord Vishnu according to the Vaishnavism tradition

Structure

The temple is located at an altitude of 2,800 meters above sea level and is surrounded by lush green forests and mountains. The temple has a pagoda-style architecture with intricate carvings on its walls and doors. Inside the temple, there is a 12-feet-tall idol of Lord Vishnu along with idols of other Hindu deities. The temple complex also has a dharamshala (rest house) for the devotees.

Getting There

Sworgadwari Temple is located in Pyuthan district of Nepal, about 250 km from Kathmandu. The nearest airport is in Pokhara, which is about 170 km away from the temple. From Pokhara, one can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the temple. The journey from Pokhara to the temple takes around 8-10 hours by road. It is also possible to trek to the temple from nearby villages like Bijuwar and Khurkot.
