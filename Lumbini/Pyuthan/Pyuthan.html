Pyuthan

Pyuthan is a district located in the mid-western region of Nepal. It is one of the seventy-seven districts of Nepal and covers an area of approximately 1,309 square kilometers. Pyuthan district is bordered by Rolpa district to the east, Rukum district to the north and west, and Salyan district to the south. Pyuthan is predominantly a rural area with agriculture being the main source of income for the locals. The district is known for its production of rice, wheat, maize, and millet. In addition to agriculture, some people in Pyuthan are also involved in animal husbandry and trade.Pyuthan is divided into nine local units, including six rural municipalities and three municipalities. The three municipalities are Pyuthan Municipality, Sworgadwari Municipality, and Jhimruk Municipality. The six rural municipalities are Sarumarani Rural Municipality, Madi Rural Municipality, Gaumukhi Rural Municipality, Nawalparasi Rural Municipality, Naubahini Rural Municipality, and Pyuthan Rural Municipality. The district headquarters of Pyuthan is Pyuthan Municipality, which is located in the southern part of the district. The district is known for its natural beauty, including the Jhimruk Khola, which is a popular destination for rafting enthusiasts. Pyuthan is also home to the Sworgadwari Temple, which is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus. The population of Pyuthan district is predominantly made up of ethnic groups such as the Magar, Chhetri, and Brahmin. The main languages spoken in the district are Nepali and Magar, while the main religions practiced are Hinduism and Buddhism.
