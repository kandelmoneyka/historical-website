Ganesh Temple

The Ganesh Temple of Banke, also known as the Banke Bihari Temple, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesh located in Banke district of Nepal. Here's some information about its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The Ganesh Temple of Banke is believed to have been built in the 16th century by a Nepalese king named Pratap Malla. It is considered to be one of the oldest and most important temples in the Banke district.

Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, Lord Ganesh is the god of wisdom, prosperity, and good fortune. He is also known as the remover of obstacles and is worshiped at the beginning of any auspicious occasion. It is believed that praying at the Ganesh Temple of Banke can bring good luck and blessings to the devotees.

Structure

The Ganesh Temple of Banke is a beautiful two-story temple with intricate carvings and decorations. The temple has a rectangular shape with a sloping roof and is built in the traditional Nepalese pagoda style. The temple has a small shrine room where the idol of Lord Ganesh is placed. The temple also has a courtyard and a separate room for the priests.

Getting There

The Ganesh Temple of Banke is located in the heart of the Banke district and is easily accessible by road. The nearest airport is Nepalgunj Airport, which is about 17 kilometers away from the temple. Visitors can also take a bus or hire a taxi from Kathmandu or other major cities in Nepal to reach the temple. Overall, the Ganesh Temple of Banke is an important religious site in Nepal and attracts a large number of devotees throughout the year.
