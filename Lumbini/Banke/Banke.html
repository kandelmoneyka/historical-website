Banke

Banke is a district located in the Province No. 5 of Nepal. It covers an area of 2,337 square kilometers and has a population of approximately 500,000 people. Banke is a diverse district with a rich cultural heritage, a strong agricultural economy, and several popular tourist destinations.The district headquarters of Banke is the city of Nepalgunj, which is one of the largest cities in western Nepal. Nepalgunj is located near the border with India and is an important hub for trade and commerce between the two countries. The city is also home to several important institutions, including the Nepalgunj Medical College and the Bheri Zonal Hospital. The district of Banke is known for its diverse ethnic and cultural heritage. It is home to people of various ethnic groups, including Tharu, Magar, Brahmin, Chhetri, and Newar. The district is also known for its natural beauty, including the Bardiya National Park, which is a popular destination for tourists interested in wildlife and nature. In terms of economy, Banke is an important agricultural district, with a significant portion of the population engaged in farming and related activities. The district is also home to several industries, including textile, food processing, and pharmaceuticals. The district's proximity to India also makes it an important hub for trade and commerce.
