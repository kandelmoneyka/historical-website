Saraswati Temple

The Saraswati Temple of Banke is a Hindu temple located in the town of Banke in Nepal. It is dedicated to the goddess Saraswati, the Hindu goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.

History

The history of the Saraswati Temple of Banke is unclear, but it is believed to have been built during the Licchavi period (3rd to 7th century AD). The temple was rebuilt in the 17th century by King Pratap Malla of Kathmandu. The temple has undergone several renovations over the years.

Mythology

According to Hindu mythology, Saraswati is the daughter of Lord Brahma, the creator of the universe. She is often depicted as a beautiful woman wearing a white sari and holding a veena (a musical instrument). Saraswati is revered as the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. She is also considered to be the patron goddess of students and scholars.

Structure

The Saraswati Temple of Banke is a beautiful temple built in the traditional Nepali style of architecture. The temple is made of brick and stone and is adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple has a single entrance and is surrounded by a wall.

Getting There

The Saraswati Temple of Banke is located in the town of Banke, which is in the Banke District of Nepal. The nearest airport is the Nepalgunj Airport, which is about 15 km from the temple. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a bus to Banke. The temple is also easily accessible by road. Buses and taxis are available from major cities in Nepal to Banke.
