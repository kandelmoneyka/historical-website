Bageshwori Temple

Bageshwori Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in the Banke district of Nepal. Here's some information about its history, mythology, structure, and how to get there:

History

The exact history of Bageshwori Temple is not known, but it is believed to have been built during the medieval period. The temple was destroyed during the 1934 Nepal-Bihar earthquake and was later rebuilt in 1937.

Mythology

According to local beliefs, Bageshwori Temple is considered to be one of the most powerful and holy places in Nepal. It is believed that worshipping at this temple can bring good fortune, success, and prosperity. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Bageshwori, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati, the wife of Lord Shiva. The temple also has a statue of Lord Ganesh, the son of Lord Shiva and Parvati.

Structure

Bageshwori Temple is a beautiful temple made of stone and brick. The temple has a pagoda-style architecture and has a height of around 18 meters. The temple is decorated with intricate carvings and sculptures, and the inner sanctum is adorned with gold-plated images of Goddess Bageshwori and Lord Ganesh.

Getting There

Bageshwori Temple is located in the heart of Nepal's Banke district, and is easily accessible by road from major cities like Kathmandu, Pokhara, and Chitwan. The nearest airport to the temple is Nepalgunj Airport, which is around 20 kilometers away. Buses and taxis are available from Nepalgunj to the temple.
